Research

My research interests include deep learning, generative models, approximate inference, and computer vision, with a special focus on the intersection of Machine Learning and Computational Neuroscience.

Currently, I am fascinated by the Free Energy Principle proposed by Prof. Karl Friston. This theory describes with mathematical precision how the brain conserves energy by minimizing surprise, and has been successfully applied to account for many aspects of perception, learning, and action. The central goal of my research, inspired by those seminal works in computational neuroscience, is to develop interpretable models and algorithms that can address computer vision problems effectively and efficiently like humans.
